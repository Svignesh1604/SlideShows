Run-Out
In cricket, "run out" refers to a method of dismissal where a batsman is declared out because they fail to reach the crease at the opposite end of the pitch safely before the fielding team can dislodge the bails with the ball.

This can happen either by direct throw or a fielder breaking the stumps with the ball while the batsman is outside the crease, attempting a run.

Batsmen are crucial to their team's success, requiring strong hand-eye coordination, concentration, and the ability to play various shots.

Bowling
Bowlers, in the context of cricket, are players who deliver the ball to the batsman in an attempt to get them out. Key skills for bowlers include accuracy, variations in pace or spin, and the ability to read the batsman's weaknesses.

Bowlers can be classified into different types based on their bowling style, such as fast bowlers who rely on speed, and spin bowlers who rely on turning the ball. Their main objectives include taking wickets, restricting the flow of runs, and creating pressure on the batting side

Fielding
Fielding in cricket involves players on the fielding team positioning themselves strategically to prevent the batting team from scoring runs and to get batters out

They can catch balls hit in the air, stop and return balls hit along the ground, and attempt to run batters out by hitting the stumps with the ball before the batter reaches the crease. Effective fielding requires agility, quick reflexes, strong throwing accuracy, and good anticipation.

Stumbing
In cricket, "stumping" refers to a method of dismissing a batsman. It occurs when the wicketkeeper, standing behind the batsman, removes the bails from the stumps while the batsman is out of their crease and not attempting a run.

This typically happens when the batsman misses the ball, and their foot is outside the crease, giving the wicketkeeper a chance to quickly gather the ball and dislodge the bails. Stumping requires quick reflexes and precision from the wicketkeeper.
